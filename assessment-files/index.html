The Trouble with Bears

By Evan Wild

Tall, lumbering, angry, dangerous. The real live bears of this world are proud, independent creatures, self-serving and always on the hunt for food. Nothing like the bears you see on TV, like Baloo from the documentary “The Jungle Book.”

So what are bears really like, and why does the world's media portray them with such a skewed vision? In this article, we try to answer those questions and give you a real insight into the life of the bear.

Types of Bear

Bears come in two varieties — large and medium. You don't get small bears. If you saw a small bear, it was probably a baby bear (a cub) from another species.

Bears can also be classified by their habitat. Both large and medium bears can live in urban areas or the countryside. The table below gives you some useful facts about bears.

Comparison of wild and urban bear characteristics
Bear Type Coat Adult size Habitat Lifespan Diet
Wild Brown or black 1.4 to 2.8 meters Woods and forests 25 to 28 years Fish, meat, plants
Urban North Face 18 to 22 Condos and coffee shops 20 to 32 years Starbucks, sushi

Habitats and Eating Habits

Wild bears eat a variety of meat, fish, fruit, and nuts. They usually hunt for food in woodland or rivers, but will occasionally search farms and homes for food.

Urban bears have largely abandoned the old ways. They will hunt if necessary, but they prefer easily accessible urban food like dumpsters and fast-food outlets.

Mating Rituals

Bears are romantic creatures and look for mates they can spend the rest of their lives with. They prepare their homes with decorations or music depending on their habitat.
